## DOCUMENTATION REQUIRED FOR A WEDDING IN KENYA

The documentation outlined below will be required for your wedding in Kenya.  Please note that complete and clear photocopies of the documents will be required no later than 10 weeks prior to travel. Fax copies are not accepted.

If documents are not received by this deadline we may not have enough time to complete the necessary arrangements.

## PLEASE RETURN CHECK LIST WITH ALL RELEVANT DOCUMENTS

- [ ] Passport with a minimum of 6 months' validity.

Birth certificates (both short and long certificates are acceptable).

- [ ] Full address of your country of residence for both individuals.

- [ ] Details of occupation for both individuals.

- [ ] Fathers' names (Full names, address and occupation).

- [ ] If either of you are divorced, proof of the Decree Absolute is required.  This document must clearly show the Court stamp.

- [ ] If either of you are widowed, the former marriage certificate and death certificate of the spouse will be required.

- [ ] If either of you have changed names by deed poll, you must provide proof that the process was duly followed.

- [ ] If either of you have been adopted, adoption papers will be required.

- [ ] An affidavit stating that neither of you have been married previously or that you have been married and divorced (see above if divorced or widowed).  This must be prepared in your country prior to travel.

- [ ] Any relevant documentation you hold which is in any language other than English must be officially translated and be accompanied by the original.

NOTE: You must carry all the original copies of aforementioned documents.
