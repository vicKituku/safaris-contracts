## Laikipia

Ol Doinyo Lemboro is located in Central Laikipia with the most spectacular variety in landscape. The low-lying land around the Ewaso Narok River is bordered by vibrant ridgelines dotted with ancient dryland forest remnants. In contrast, the plateaus of the western ridges which advertise the edge of the great Rift Valley, form flat grassy plains with brilliant escarpment views.

Within this 20,000-acre Landscape, Laikipia Expeditions has three camps; Laikipia Wilderness Main Camp, Palm Camp and River Camp. All three Camps are designed to provide an adventurous safari experience while contributing to the local area economically and environmentally. We proudly consider our camps designed for an authentic experience, not luxury.

<!-- image -->

Travelling to Laikipia can be done by driving directly from Nairobi for about 5 hours or flying to Nanyuki Airstrip or Loisaba Airstrip where we can collect you. The drive from either airstrip takes about 2 hours. If you would like to charter a flight to camp, our closest small strip is Sosian where we can collect you for a 30-minute drive to camp.

90% of the Laikipia expedition staff are recruited locally. Exceptional chefs oversee the vegetable and herb garden and purchase local produce which are cooked using a specialised, eco-friendly wood-burning stove or over an open fire using 'elephant-damaged wood'. These skilled chefs produce gorgeous buffet-style meals with a hard-to-beat, camping hint of smoky flavour. All dietary requirements are catered for across all of our small eco-friendly safari camps.

At Laikipia Wilderness, we are proud of our small environmental footprint. We recycle everything possible, using compost and greywater for the garden. Our local community partner turns our empty wine bottles into glasses for use at camp. The products in our small shop are bought from local women's groups. Electricity is exclusively solar and we use digestive systems for waste water.

Central areas of each camp have Wi-Fi that enables access to the rest of the world, we recommend charging heavier batteries in the central areas of the main camp to avoid damaging them, and smaller voltage items are chargeable in the rooms.

## Laikipia Wilderness Main Camp

Nestling on a hillside above the Ewaso Narok River, Laikipia Wilderness Camp provides a unique, adventurous safari experience where people prefer to stay longer and enjoy this family-owned camp.

The rooms are carefully sited to invite both couples who seek a private, secluded stay and families of all ages. Ajoined tents with a shared bathroom and veranda enable families to keep an eye on little ones, yet soak up the wilderness experience.

Six tented rooms complement a central dining area with indoor and outdoor sitting areas giving snug warmth and views of the stunning Laikipia Landscape. Plentiful books and board games enable reference and relaxation. Traditional and beautifully rustic; each bedroom and ensuite bathroom is individually designed using local materials, sustainable soaps and products. Cool, private verandas invite peace and relaxation while you enjoy the vista and the wildlife.

On top of a rock above the main camp, a swinging bench offers a 360-degree view of the Laikipia landscape with the impressive skyline of Mount Kenya on clear days. Feel free to grab your book and enjoy the tranquillity of this unique viewpoint. The owner's dogs do live in the main camp helping to make the atmosphere feel like home.

Our four spacious, fully furnished tented rooms are designed to ensure comfort for our guests whilst not deterring from the wildness of the Mutara landscape. Guests are provided with the authentic experience of the Kenyan wilderness, from its bird songs to its calling lions, without compromising the comfort expected of an East African tented safari. Our open-air en-suite bathrooms bring a unique symbiosis with the natural beauty of the landscape. Traditional bucket showers warmed upon request, pair perfectly with the soft morning breeze, whilst our modern flush toilets ensure the comforts of home without compromising the camp's classic off-grid appeal.

The camp is centred around an inviting dining and sitting area, where the essence of the African safari experience comes to life. Our dining area perfectly blends the atmosphere of a family dinner with quality meals, lovingly prepared by our experienced kitchen team, capable of warming any chilly evening! All our home-style meals are prepared with fresh, local produce from trusted suppliers, allowing us to support local communities whilst guaranteeing the quality our guests come to expect. All meals are served buffet style around a central table, where our trained hosts and waiting team are present to ensure all needs are met, allowing conversation, laughter and stories of the day's adventures to flow freely over a drink of choice. Our cosy mess tent accommodates the perfect space for relaxation, while the outdoor fireplace is the ideal setting to finish the evening with drinks and stories under the stars.

Under the expert management of Steve Carey and Graeme Lemon, our camp boasts over sixty years of combined experience in unique, hand-crafted safaris throughout the continent. Our guides are expertly trained and share unbridled passion and knowledge of the natural world. We believe that every adventure is a unique opportunity to connect, to connect with our team under a magical firelit evening or to connect with nature itself, in which we are blessed to be surrounded.

## Laikipia Wildlife

The wildlife in Laikipia is extraordinary. The area is still wild and largely unfenced, which enables game to move around the landscape, following the rains. Home to a huge variety,  from Lions to Dik-dik, laikipia has become a stronghold for the more unusual species including more than 70% of the world's remaining Grevy's zebra as well as other species including reticulated giraffe, Beisa oryx, zorilla, striped hyena, aardwolf, wild dog, and melanistic leopard. Large permanent herds of elephant, lion, leopard, spotted hyena, common zebra, warthog, hippo, buffalo, and many different antelope species. Our guides will help you identify and enjoy the plentiful (recorded 350) species of birds. Be sure to use your Laikipia Wilderness Booklet during your stay which has an extensive mammal and bird list for you to tick off.

## Wild dog

At times we have some of the most incredible wild dog sightings. One pack, in particular, has been monitored for over ten years. This was more recently named the 'Phoenix Pack' as they recovered from a severe outbreak of canine distemper in 2017. The wild dogs are not always in the immediate area, but they often hunt nearby and can be found daily at times. Chances of seeing them have been good and we are delighted to see the population expanding again. When booking your safari please check in with the reservations team about the best time for seeing wild dogs.

## Leopard

Originally captured on camera by Will Burrard Lucas in 2019, Laikipia has become famous for sightings of black leopards. These melanistic cats are extremely rare and we are privileged to have them, one female was born and raised less than 100m from camp. She is seen fairly regularly and is especially relaxed around vehicles which provides us with the most impressive sightings. We are seeing her about 4 nights a week but she is a wild animal and can be unpredictable as a result.

We limit the number of vehicles at a sighting and the amount of time spent with the leopard. Those with a Black Leopard private vehicle (BLPV) or a Black Leopard shared vehicle (BLSV) will be given one of the priority slots at a sightings. Please book and pay for your Black Leopard vehicle in advance. Single Photographers are encouraged to use the BLSV. Kindly note that all black leopard-focused drives will be restricted to 6 am and 8 pm respectively, in order to allow the leopard time to be a leopard. The spotlights have also been fitted with red filters. In case of any signs of stress, your guide has the right to end a sighting at any point. Kindly respect the leopard at all sightings. Black Leopard Private Vehicle is charged at $350 per night Black Leopard Shared Vehicle is charged at $100 per person per night (1 booking per row). This includes afternoon, evening and morning game drives, not transfers. Black leopard vehicles should be included at the start of your booking. If you do not feel the need for a BL vehicle but you would still like to be given the chance to see the black leopard then normal vehicles will be rotated into a sighting as/if/when there is space. For more information, please have a look at our website.

Booking a black leopard vehicle does not guarantee sightings as she is a wild animal. This is only for a priority slot at a sighting where the number of vehicles is limited. Not necessary for all guests.
