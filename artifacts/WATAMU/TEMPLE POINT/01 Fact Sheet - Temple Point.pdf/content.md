## Location

Temple  Point Resort is an oasis of adventure  and relaxation  with  direct  access  to  two  pristine,  fine  sandy beaches.  It  is  uniquely  nestled  between  the  Watamu Marine National Park and the birds paradise Mida Creek on the top of the peninsula of Watamu where the Indian Ocean meets the Mida Creek.

The MOI Mombasa International Airport is 120km away and the Malindi Airport is 20km from the hotel.

Tsavo East National Park is just a 1.5h drive away.

## Style

Spread over a 50,000 sqm botanical garden, 16 individually built chalets highlight the beauty of traditional  Kenyan  coastal  architecture  featuring  white exteriors  and  bold  makuti  roofs.  The  lush  creekfront property is adorned by colorful plants and tropical flowers.  The  heart  of  Temple  Point  is  found  at  the modern  Bazaar  featuring  a  pool,  boutique,  bar,  open kitchen  restaurant,  weekend  cinema,  co-working  space, and occasional events, styled with colorful hanging glass lanterns, surrounded by palm trees, fragrant frangipani's, and playful monkeys.

## Rooms

The resort has 64 rooms. Each of the chalets houses 3 - 8 rooms, provided with either a shared terrace or balcony.

All  rooms  offer  a  modern  bathroom  with  hairdryer, toiletries and body care products, air conditioner, mosquito net, safe, and ceiling fan.

All rooms are nonsmoking, some rooms are dog friendly.

## Diving

Our on-site dive center by Extra Divers offers basic and advanced SSI and PADI certifications and daily dive trips. Explore  the    underwater  world  of  the  Watamu  Marine National Park where divers discover its still faultless coral reef  with  colorful  fish,  rays,  whales,  dolphins,  octopus, 140 species of coral, and much more!

The  average  water  temp  is  between  24-28°C,  with depths  between  8  and  40  meters,  visibility  5-20m  and dive  sites  being  between  20  and  40  minutes  away  by boat.  The  dive  center  offers  daily  morning  trips  with double dives, as well as afternoon &amp; night dives.

The dive center is closed in May and June.

## Safaris and Excursions

## Safari

Home  to  54  National  Parks,  Kenya  is  a  treasure  for nature and animal lovers.

The  gate  of  Tsavo  East  National  Park  can  be  reached within an hour and a half, making it a great location for a quick  1.5  days  safari.  Lumo  Conservancy  near  Tsavo West  is  situated  right  in  the  elephant  migratory  path between the two parks, and we arrange custom itineraries to all other National Parks.

## Excursions

Gede Ruins, a historical and cultural site within Watamu, offers visitors the opportunity to learn about the history and  previous  economic  classes  of  Watamu.  Arabuko Sokoke  Forest  offers  visitors  the  opportunity  to  come face  to  face  with  the  elephants  of  the  forest  while enjoying  sunset  snacks  at  the  watering  hole.  Snorkel  in the  Mida  Creek  or  watch  dolphins  from  the  netted hammock aboard the resort's boat or intimate dhow.

## Climate

The climate is tropical with high air humidity and cooling monsoon  winds.  The  temperature  averages  between 25°C and 30°C and up to 32°C from November to April.

In Kenya, the long rainy season in May and June differs from  the  short rains  season  at  end  of  October  to December.  There  may  be  no  rain  in  the  short  season while  during  the  long  rain  season  there  may  be  short heavy rains.
